A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

The Missile Man of India is a title given to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam his significant contributions to India's missile development programs. He played a crucial role in the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles. Dr. Kalam was also the 11th President of India (2002–2007) and was widely respected for his vision for India's scientific and technological advancement. His contributions to India's space and defense programs earned him numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.

Biographies

  • Scientist – Played a key role in developing India’s Agni and Prithvi missiles, earning the title "Missile Man of India".
  • President –Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007) and was known as the "People’s President".
  • Author – Wrote inspiring books like "Wings of Fire", sharing his journey from a small-town boy to a renowned scientist.
  • Teacher – Dedicated his life to educating and inspiring youth, even calling himself a teacher first.
  • Visionary – Developed "Vision 2020", a roadmap for India's development in science, technology, and economy.
